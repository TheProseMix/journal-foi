# Saag Aloo + Gobi Aloo

The curry is basically saag aloo and gobi aloo, mixed together.

## Ingredients

Serves 3

* 3 large potatoes; cut into cubes of about 1-2cm per side
* 1 large brown onion; cut in half and then finely sliced
* 400g cauliflower florets (or cauliflower and broccoli florets); cut
  into relatively small florets
* 400g tinned chopped tomatoes
* 150g baby spinach leaves
* 2-3tbsp black mustard seeds
* 1tbsp ground fenugreek
* 1tbsp ground turmeric
* 2tsp chilli powder
* Optional: 1 red chilli, cut along its length
* Handful of dried curry leaves
* Table salt
* Ground pepper
* Vegetable oil

## Method

### Curry Gravy

1. Cover the bottom of a large pan with vegetable oil and add the
   mustard seeds at a high heat.

2. The mustard seeds will start to pop and become aromatic, when they're
   ready. At which point, add the sliced onions, turn the heat down and
   brown.

3. When the onions have browned off, throw in the curry leaves and stir
   in. This is also the time for the red chilli, if you're using it. Fry
   for a further minute, or so.

4. Add the fenugreek, turmeric and chilli powder. Mix thoroughly and fry
   for a minute, or so.

5. Reduce the heat and add the tomatoes. Stir together with table salt,
   to taste.

6. Add the cauliflower florets (raw), stir until they are covered in the
   gravy and then reduce the heat and cover.

The longer this simmers, the better and the more the cauliflower will
cook. Don't overdo it, though, or the cauliflower will go mushy. The
time it takes to sauté the potatoes should be enough.

### Sauté Potatoes

1. Boil enough heavily salted water to submerse the potatoes. Add the
   potatoes and, when the water starts boiling again, parboil for about
   3-4 minutes.

2. Drain the potatoes and leave aside.

3. Heat a large amount of oil, on a high heat, in a sauté pan.

4. Add the potatoes, season with quite a lot of salt -- it's surprising
   how much you need -- and pepper. Then mix so the seasoning and oil is
   well distributed.

5. Leave the potatoes cooking, shaking the pan every minute or so to
   avoid them sticking. After about 7-10 minutes, they should be browned
   on one side. At which point, you can turn them and repeat the
   process (probably with slightly less time, on each turn).

6. Repeat step 5 until the potatoes are mostly golden; they don't have
   to be uniform -- that's not really doable, when shallow frying -- but
   they should be close enough. You may need to add more salt and pepper
   to taste: The final result should have crispy skins and be slightly
   salty to taste. (Don't worry: This saltiness will be lost, when added
   to the gravy).

7. Put aside, off the heat, once done.

### Putting It All Together

1. Remove the lid from the gravy and stir in the spinach on a low heat.

2. Once the spinach is fully incorporated, add the potatoes (and their
   oil) and carefully stir them in. Don't be too rough, or the potatoes
   might lose their texture. (You might want to hold some of the
   potatoes back, to ensure you get some crispy bits.)

3. Serve. Remove the red chilli, if you used it (or use it as garnish).
